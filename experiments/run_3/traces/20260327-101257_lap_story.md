## Chapter 1: Cold Start on the Long Straight (Steps 1–28 | 0–14%)

The episode opens with every telemetry field reading `0.0` — position, heading, speed, track width, all zero. This is the simulator's initialization state, not a real location. Starved of valid data, Claude does the correct thing: it ignores the numbers entirely and reads the camera image, seeing "a track curving to the left." It starts the car with a conservative +10° left steer at 1.5 m/s.

One step later, real telemetry arrives. The car is eastbound on a long straight at y ≈ 0.683 m, perfectly centered. For 26 steps the LLM computes near-zero deltas (< 5°) and correctly commands `steering_angle=0` at full speed. The car barely drifts — `distance_from_center` never exceeds 0.012 m. Textbook straight-line driving.

> ⚠️ **The meta-problem**: Every single one of these "simple straight" steps still takes 12–16 seconds of inference. At 1.5 m/s the simulated car covers 22 cm per second. The AI director takes 15 seconds between decisions. In wall-clock terms, this "racing" lap will consume over an hour.

## Chapter 2: The First Turn — Self-Correcting a Mistake (Steps 29–62 | 15–34%)

Delta creeps positive at step 29 (+4.6°). The LLM begins applying gentle left steer, building from +5° to +15° over eight steps as the track sweeps into a full 180° left arc carrying the car from east-facing to west-facing.

**Notable inconsistency (Step 30 knowledge)**: After just one step of turning, the LLM updates its running knowledge to: *"Big right curve begins around WP22-30… Prepare to steer right when delta goes negative."* This is factually wrong — the curve is a **left** turn (counter-clockwise, bearing 0° → 180°). And yet the LLM immediately ignores this note, correctly following the *positive* delta with *positive* (left) steering. By step 60 the knowledge is tacitly self-corrected to *"Left curve spans WP27-44 requiring sustained positive steering ~15°."* The erroneous fact died quietly without causing harm, but it was a genuine error of compass interpretation.

The arc itself is executed well: 33 steps at 1.5 m/s, delta consistently 28–35°, steering locked at +15°, distance from center peaking at just 0.067 m. Clean.

The western return section involves gentle curves and one significant right-steering episode (steps 70–83) as the car, heading west at ~-178°, must correct rightward toward waypoints tracking slightly south. Delta runs -8° to -30°, handled with -5° to -15° steering.

## Chapter 4: First Major Hairpin — Into the Abyss (Steps 122–155 | 69–77%)

The track turns sharply southward. Delta escalates fast:

| Step | Progress | Delta  | Speed | Steering |
|------|----------|--------|-------|----------|
| 122  | 68.8%    | +18°   | 1.5   | +10      |
| 123  | 69.2%    | +34°   | 1.5   | +15      |
| 124  | 69.6%    | **+56°**   | **0.75**  | **+25**      |
| 125  | 70.1%    | **+77°**   | 0.75  | +25      |
| 130  | 71.2%    | **+148°**  | 0.75  | +25      |

From step 124 through step 155 — **32 consecutive steps** — the car runs at minimum speed (0.75 m/s) with maximum left lock (+25°). During this crawl the car drifts rightward to **0.299 m from center** — just 0.080 m (8 cm) from falling off the right edge.

**Highlight (Step 130) — waypoint wraparound**: The LLM's lookahead formula is `(nearest_waypoints[1]+5) % 121`. Deep in the hairpin, this points at WP90 at coordinates **(6.667, 2.678)** — nearly back at the *start line* — when the car is at **(0.672, 4.270)**. The bearing to WP90 from here is -14.9°; the car heading is -162.9°; delta = **+148°**. The target waypoint has wrapped around the circuit. The LLM dutifully reports "large left correction needed" and keeps turning left — physically correct, but it is essentially navigating by a target that is already a quarter-lap behind it.

**Highlight (Step 153) — explicit image/math contradiction**: The LLM writes verbatim: *"Track is straight ahead in image but waypoint analysis shows large +67.6° delta requiring continued maximum left steering."* It sees the disagreement, calls it out, and correctly trusts the math over the visual. The image perspective implied straight; the waypoint math knew better. The right call.

## Chapter 5: The Second Hairpin — Right Then Violently Left (Steps 156–186 | 77–88%)

At step 156, delta snaps from the hairpin's large positives to -6.3°. One step after 32 steps of 0.75/+25, the LLM is back to 1.5 m/s with -5° steering. Instant mode switch.

Within two steps (158) delta is already -35.7° — a **right** hairpin now. The car applies -20° to -25° steering at speed 0.75–1.0 for steps 158–167. It reaches 0.239 m left of center at step 165 (0.14 m from the left edge).

Then comes the strangest moment of the entire lap:

**Highlight (Steps 176–177) — delta nearly ±180°**:
- Step 176: `bearing≈+69.4°, delta≈+177.1°` → steering +25, speed 0.75
- Step 177: `bearing≈+72.1°, delta≈+178.2°` → steering +25, speed 0.75

Target WP106 is at (1.034, 1.959) — almost **directly behind** the car at (0.948, 1.731), heading -107.7° (west-southwest). The bearing to WP106 is northeast (+69°); the heading is -107°; so delta = 69 − (−107) = **+176°**. The math is correct. But the LLM has a target waypoint pointing almost 180° backward and interprets "large positive delta, steer hard left" — which happens to be the right physical response for the hairpin, even though the reasoning is geometrically incoherent. A correct answer derived from a near-degenerate computation.

## Chapter 6: Recovery Then Another Explosion (Steps 187–208 | 88–96%)

The car exits the second hairpin cleanly. Steps 187–188 show delta unwinding (+21° → +14.4°), soft +5° corrections, speed back to 1.5.

**Inconsistency (Steps 188→189)**: One step after a gentle +5° correction at 88.5%, the next step snaps to **-15°** steering. Delta flips from +14.4° to -22.4° as the LLM explains *"WP110 is now slightly behind/below the car's heading."* The waypoint-ahead calculation has crossed a threshold and WP110 is now below the car's bearing, flipping the required correction. One step later at -37.2° it applies -20°. The car hammers right at step 190 — 1.0 m/s, -20° — then calms back down by step 191 (-14°, -5°). A three-step oscillation around a waypoint transition.

Steps 196–208 produce another "exploding delta" sequence:
